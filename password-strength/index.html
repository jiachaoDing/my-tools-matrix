    FAQ

    How do you calculate password strength?

    Strength is calculated using Information Entropy. We analyze the "pool size" (number of possible characters) and the length. We also check for common patterns and complexity requirements like uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

    Is my password safe here?

    Yes. This tool is built with a "Privacy First" philosophy. No data is ever uploaded or saved. You can even disconnect your internet and the tool will still work.

    What is a "good" entropy score?

    Generally, an entropy score above 60 bits is considered "Good" for most personal accounts, while 80+ bits is "Strong" and 100+ is "Very Strong" (military-grade).

    Why does crack time vary?

    Crack time depends on how many guesses an attacker can make per second. We show estimates for both standard consumer hardware and highly optimized supercomputer clusters.

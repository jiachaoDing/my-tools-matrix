FAQ

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to accomplish its most basic (basal) life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulation, and cell production. It is calculated while you are at complete rest.

How is BMR different from TDEE?

BMR is your baseline calorie need at rest. Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories you burn in a day, including physical activity and the thermic effect of food.

Which BMR formula do you use?

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is widely considered by the scientific and nutrition community as the most reliable standard for healthy adults.

Mbps vs. MB/s

Internet Service Providers (ISPs) typically advertise speeds in Mbps (Megabits per second). However, most applications like Steam or browsers show download progress in MB/s (Megabytes per second). Since 1 Byte equals 8 Bits, a 100 Mbps connection actually downloads at roughly 12.5 MB/s maximum.

Speed FAQ

Why is my download taking longer than estimated?
Estimates can be affected by server-side throttling, ISP instability, other devices on your network using bandwidth, or signal interference if using Wi-Fi.
What is "network overhead"?
Network overhead refers to the extra data sent along with your file (like IP headers and handshake packets) to ensure it reaches your device intact. This typically reduces effective speeds by about 10-15%.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why convert GIF to MP4?
MP4 videos are typically 80-90% smaller than GIFs while offering better quality and smoother playback. GIF format stores each frame individually, while MP4 uses modern video codecs (H.264) that efficiently compress by storing only differences between frames. MP4s also support millions of colors versus GIF's 256-color limit.
Is my data secure?
Absolutely! All conversion happens locally in your browser using FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly. Your GIF files never leave your device and are never uploaded to any server, ensuring complete privacy and security.
Which quality setting should I choose?
Medium quality is perfect for most use cases, offering an excellent balance between file size and visual quality. Use high quality for professional content or important presentations. Low quality is ideal when file size is critical, such as for messaging apps or slow connections.
Why does the first conversion take time?
The first conversion requires downloading FFmpeg.wasm (~30MB), a powerful video processing library compiled to WebAssembly. This is a one-time download that gets cached by your browser, making all subsequent conversions much faster. It's the price we pay for complete privacy and no server uploads.
